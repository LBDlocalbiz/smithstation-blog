How to Choose Your Storage Unit Size

Step 1: Create Detailed Item Inventory

List everything for storage by category. Group similar items and estimate space requirements for each group. Use our proven inventory checklist: - Furniture: Measure largest pieces (length x width x height) - Boxes: Count and categorize (books are heavier, clothes are bulkier) - Appliances: Include dimensions and access requirements - Special items: Artwork, mirrors, mattresses (require vertical space)

Step 2: Evaluate Access Requirements

Frequent access (weekly or monthly): Choose larger units with walking space and organized layout zones. Long-term storage (6+ months): Allows more efficient packing in smaller spaces with strategic stacking. Seasonal access (quarterly): Plan for easy access to front-stored seasonal items.

Step 3: Plan for Future Growth

Military families typically accumulate 10-15% more belongings annually. Growing businesses often need 25-30% additional space within two years. Starting larger saves moving costs and rental truck fees later.

Step 4: Assess Climate Needs
